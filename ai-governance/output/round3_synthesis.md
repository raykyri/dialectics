# Round 3 Synthesis: The Invitation Thesis

## The Reframe

The design/deployment binary dissolves when you see that the thing being offered to communities isn't a tool or even a practice — it's an **invitation to collectively investigate a shared problem.** The investigation IS the deployment IS the practice design.

## The Thesis

The real distinction isn't design-first vs. deploy-first. It's **scaling vs. learning.** A first deployment that treats itself as learning — small community, specific problem, honest evaluation, community as co-investigator — is what both careful-design and build-now advocates actually prescribe. A deployment that treats itself as scaling is what both rightly warn against.

An invitation is a lightweight structure for collective investigation — simple enough to start immediately, open enough that the community shapes it, honest enough that it presents itself as an experiment, not a solution.

## Key Claims

### 1. The design/deployment binary is false
Practices don't exist prior to enactment. But premature scaling produces governance theater. The resolution: start immediately with specific communities on specific problems, framed as collective investigation, not product launch.

### 2. Power direction matters
Every cautionary tale (COMPAS, PredPol, Facebook) involved tools deployed BY powerful institutions ON less powerful people. Community self-governance tools reverse this — though tool designers remain unacknowledged governance actors whose choices must be legible.

### 3. Invitations, not platforms
The thing to offer communities isn't a tool to be deployed but an invitation to collectively investigate their governance problems. The investigation is the deployment is the practice design.

## Concrete Invitations

- **Open source maintainers** managing AI contributions: structured process for collectively evaluating contribution quality, AI surfaces patterns and maintains shared memory of quality standards
- **Research communities** facing replication concerns: collectively map the evidence landscape, AI handles mechanical synthesis, community does judgment
- **Climate coordination networks**: jointly investigate what adaptation strategies municipalities have tried, investigation IS the coordination mechanism

## Through-line Across All Three Rounds

- Round 1: The practice is the unit of cognition → design practices, not tools
- Round 2: Collective investigation IS governance → the practice is collective inquiry
- Round 3: The invitation dissolves design/deployment → start immediately, stay small, let governance emerge from investigation

## Unresolved
1. The bootstrapping problem: who extends the invitation? The inviter has power.
2. The evaluation problem: what counts as success?
3. The sustainability problem: how does an investigation become an institution?
